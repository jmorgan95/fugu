Chicken Biryani

Ingredients

  • 300g Basmati Rice
  • 1/2 lime
  • 500g chicken thighs, cut into pieces
  • 20g ginger paste
  • 25g garlic paste
  • 1.5 tsp chilli powder
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1.5 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 100g yoghurt
  • 30ml vegetable oil
  • 3 green chillies, each cut into 4 strips
  • 3cm ginger, cut into matchsticks
  • Handful mint, chopped
  • Handful coriander, chopped
  • 20g butter
  • 3 tbsp double cream
  • 1tbsp saffron infused water

Method

  1. Put the rice in a large bowl and cover generously with water. Gently move the rice around in the water to remove the starch. Allow the rice to settle, then pour off the water. Repeat twice with fresh water, then cover again and leave to soak for 45 mins
  2. Plae the chicken in a bowl. Mix all remaining ingredients together (apart from the butter, cream and saffron), add to the chicken and toss to coat. Cover and place in the fridge to marinate until you are ready to assemble the dish
  3. Heat oven to 200C / Fan 180C. Drain the rice. Boil 2l water, add lime juice and 2 tsp salt.
  4. Transfer chicken and marinade into a cooking pot, and place over a low heat to warm through while you cook the rice
  5. Tip the drained rice into the boiling water and stir well. Boil for 4-5 minutes until the rice is three quarters cooked. You can tell when the rice has reached this stage by pressing down on a grain between your fingers - is should still be slighlty firm and break into 5 pieces. Drain the rice, and place in the cooking pot on top of the chicken
  6. Warm the butter and cream in a microwave until the butter melts. Mix together then trickle over the rice, followed by the saffron water
  7. Cover tightly with two layers of foil. Place over a high heat for 2-3 minutes, until the foil puffs up and you see steam starting to escape. Put the lid on, transfer to the oven, and bake for 35-40 minutes. Let the biryani stand, still covered, for 10 minutes before serving

Source

Chef: Shamil Thakrar, Kavi Thakrar, Naved Nasir

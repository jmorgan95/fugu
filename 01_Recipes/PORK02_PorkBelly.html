Roast Pork Belly

with buttenut squash purée

Ingredients

  • 1.3kg pork belly
  • 15g fennel seeds
  • 100ml olive oil
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 sticks celery
  • 1 bulb garlic
  • 2 onions
  • 1 bunch fresh time
  • 1 bottle of white wine
  • 1 butternut squash
  • 50g butter

Method

  1. Pre-heat oven to its highest temperature
  2. Score the skin of the pork belly (try not to cut the meat), making the cuts very close together
  3. Bash the fennel seeds with a good pinch of salt in a pestle and mortar
  4. Sprinkle the fennel mixture over the pork, and rub in so that it gets into the scores and down into the meat. Drizzle the oil over and rub
  5. Roughly chop the carrots and celery then throw them into a roasting tray. Break the garlic bulb up into unpeeled cloves. Peel and chop the onions into large wedges, then toss into the tray with the sprigs of thyme
  6. Put the seasoned pork belly on top of the vegetable in the tray, then roast until the skin starts to bubble and turns golden brown (around 10-15 minutes)
  7. Reduce the oven temperature to 170C, and roast for 1 hour 30m. When the time's up, carefully open the oven door and pour in three quarters of the wine. Cook for a further hour
  8. Peel, de-seed and dice the butternut squash
  9. Melt the butter in a pan and add the butternut squash, tossing to coat and seasoning with salt and pepper
  10. Roast in the oven until soft for approximately 30-40 minutes. Remove from the oven and set aside, straining off and reserving any liquid form the roasting tray
  11. Add the butternut squash to a blender and blitz until smooth, adding a little of the reserved liquid to form a thick purée. Check the seasoning, set aside and keep warm
  12. Remove the meat carefully from the oven and test to see if it pull apart easily. If not then cook for a little longer. Allow the meat to rest while you make the gravy
  13. Put the tray of roasted vegetables directly onto your hob over a medium heat, add the flour and stir together until thick. Cook for 1 minute, then add in the remaining wine
  14. Using a potato masher, mash up the vegetables until you have a delicious thick looking sauce. Cook for 2-3 minutes on a low heat, season to taste then strain through a sieve into a jug

Source

Chef: Jamie Oliver (Pork) and Pierre Koffmann (Purée)

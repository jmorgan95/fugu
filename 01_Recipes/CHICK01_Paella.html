Chicken Paella

Ingredients

  • 0.5g saffron threads
  • A generous measure of salt
  • Extra Virgin olive oil
  • 2 chicken thighs on the bone, cut in half
  • A quarter of rabbit (or replace with more chicken or pork ribs if not using)
  • 100g runner beans
  • 50g broad beans
  • 2 artichokes, prepared into quarters
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 heaped tsp of smoked paprika
  • 1 medium sized tomato, grated
  • 1.5L chicken stock
  • 250g paella or another short-grain rice
  • 3 sprigs of rosemary
  • Lemon wedges to serve

Method

  1. Start to heat a pan. Place the saffron on a sheet of tinfoil and fold it up. Place the tin foil envelope on the heated pan for ten seconds before removing.
  2. Put a generous portion of salt on the same pan, followed by a glug of olive oil.
  3. Add the chicken thighs and rabbit to the pan. Leave to brown (15 minutes or so) then move to the sides of the pan.
  4. Add the two types of beans to the space left in the middle, followed by the artichoke quarters.
  5. Chop the garlic and add to the pan, followed by the paprika.
  6. Stir in the grated tomato and leave to cook for a short time. Add in the chicken stock and give it a little stir without disturbing the meat too much. Sprinkle in the saffron. Bring to boil.
  7. Add the rice and stir evenly around the pan. Bring to the boil again and leave for 10 minutes on the highest heat. Do not stir from this point.
  8. Reduce to a low heat for a further 6 minutes. Place the rosemary on top of the rice and cover the pan with the tin foil used earlier.
  9. Take the pan off the heat and leave to rest with the tin foil on for 5 minutes.
  10. Serve with a wedge of lemon.

Source

Chef: Omar Allibhoy
